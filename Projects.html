Comparing Sea Surface Temperature over Gulf of Guinea during Dry and Wet Year Composites of West Africa

This research investigated the influence of Sea Surface Temperature (SST) variations in the Gulf of Guinea during dry and wet year composites over West Africa. It examined precipitation patterns relative to SST, analyzed rainfall variability to identify dry and wet years, and documented spatial and temporal SST patterns from 1981–2020 using ERA5 and CRU datasets. Five seasonal and annual SST time series were evaluated using coefficient of variation for variability, and PyFerret and Python for spatial-temporal analysis. The findings provided crucial insights into climate impacts on the Gulf, supporting regional adaptation and resilience strategies.

Spatio-Temporal Analysis of Flood in Lokoja, Kogi State

This study, completed in 2023, utilized Remote Sensing and GIS to assess and analyze flood occurrences in Lokoja. It examined rainfall variability, land-use changes from 1991–2022, and flood-prone areas. Using Landsat, DEM, CRU, and POWER NASA datasets, the study rectified and geo-referenced imagery, clipped datasets to the study area, and computed a 12-month Standardized Precipitation Index for 1990–2022 to assess wetness and flood probabilities. Findings highlighted socio-economic vulnerabilities and informed strategies for sustainable flood mitigation.

Advancing Tree Planting in Oyo State: Strategic Ecosystem Restoration Research and Framework

This research assessed environmental degradation in Oyo State from 2015–2025, focusing on vegetation loss, pollution, and thermal stress. Using NDVI, LST, urban heat island analysis, air quality trends, and indigenous species evaluation, the study proposed afforestation, pollution control, sustainable urban planning, and green infrastructure strategies. It contributed actionable frameworks for policy, Gamma’s Green Agency interventions, and aligned with SDGs 3, 11, 13, and 15 to enhance resilience and community well-being.

GIS-AHP Flood Susceptibility Mapping

Integrated GIS and Analytical Hierarchy Process (AHP) to model flood susceptibility zones. The study combined 10 environmental factors such as elevation, slope, distance from river, distance from road, soil type, topographic wetness index, Normalized Difference Vegetation Index, rainfall, drainage, and land use.
